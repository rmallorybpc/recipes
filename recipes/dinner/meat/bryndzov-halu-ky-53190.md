# Summary

Bryndzové Halušky from TheMealDB imported into the planner collection.

## Ingredients

- 500g Potatoes
- 200g Plain Flour
- 1 Egg
- 1 teaspoon Salt
- 250g Bryndza cheese
- 200g Bacon
- Garnish Chives

## Instructions

1. Prepare the Dough.
2. Grate the potatoes finely using a hand grater or food processor.
3. Place the grated potatoes in a bowl and mix them with flour, egg, and salt until a sticky dough forms.
4. The consistency should be thick but pliable.
5. Cook the Dumplings.
6. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
7. Using a halušky maker (similar to a spaetzle maker), press the dough directly into the boiling water.
8. If you don’t have one, use a tilted cutting board and a knife to scrape small pieces of dough into the water.
9. Let the dumplings cook until they float to the surface, usually within 2-3 minutes.
10. Scoop them out with a slotted spoon and set aside in a large bowl.
11. Prepare the Toppings.
12. Chop the bacon into small pieces and fry in a skillet over medium heat until crispy.
13. If using a bryndza substitute, mix crumbled feta with a dollop of sour cream to mimic the tangy flavour of traditional Slovak sheep cheese.
14. Assemble the Dish.
15. Toss the cooked dumplings with the bryndza cheese (or substitute) until they’re well-coated and creamy.
16. Top with the crispy bacon and its drippings.
17. Garnish with chopped chives or parsley for an extra touch of colour and flavour.
