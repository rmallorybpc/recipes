## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 cups yellow split lentils, rinsed
- 1 large tomato, diced
- 1 large onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
- 1 teaspoon turmeric
- 1 teaspoon cumin
- 4 cups water or vegetable broth
- salt to taste
- 2 tablespoons ghee or butter for tadka
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds for tadka
- 4 cloves garlic sliced for tadka
- 2 dried red chilies for tadka
- 1/2 teaspoon garam masala for tadka
- fresh cilantro and lemon juice for serving
- basmati rice or naan for serving

## Instructions

1. Combine lentils, tomato, onion, garlic, ginger, turmeric, cumin, and water in a pot. Bring to a boil.
2. Reduce heat and simmer 25-30 minutes until lentils are completely soft and creamy.
3. Season with salt.
4. Make the tadka: heat ghee in a small pan until hot. Add cumin seeds - they should sizzle immediately. Add sliced garlic and dried chilies. Cook 1-2 minutes until golden.
5. Pour the hot tadka directly over the dal. Stir to incorporate.
6. Finish with lemon juice and cilantro. Serve over rice.

## Notes

- The sizzling tadka poured over at the end is what elevates this dish
- Yellow split lentils cook faster than red or green - no soaking needed
- Excellent reheated the next day with a splash of water
