## Ingredients

- 14 oz extra-firm tofu, pressed and cubed
- 2 cups broccoli florets
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced
- 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
- 1 cup snap peas
- 2 carrots, thinly sliced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
- 3 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon oyster sauce or hoisin sauce
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- 1 tablespoon cornstarch
- 1/4 cup water
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- cooked rice for serving
- sesame seeds for garnish

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Toss tofu with 1 tablespoon soy sauce and spread on a lined baking sheet. Bake 25 minutes until crispy.
2. Mix remaining soy sauce, hoisin, sesame oil, and cornstarch in a small bowl.
3. Heat vegetable oil in a wok over high heat. Add carrots and broccoli, stir fry 3 minutes.
4. Add bell pepper and snap peas. Stir fry 2 minutes more.
5. Add garlic and ginger. Cook 30 seconds.
6. Pour sauce over vegetables and toss. Add crispy tofu.
7. Serve over rice with sesame seeds.

## Notes

- Press tofu for at least 30 minutes for maximum crispiness
- Any firm vegetable works here - use what is in season
- Add a drizzle of chili oil for heat
