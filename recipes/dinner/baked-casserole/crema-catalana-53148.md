# Summary

## Ingredients

- 400ml Milk
- 150ml Double Cream
- 1 Cinnamon Stick
- 1 Orange Zest
- 1 Lemon Zest
- 7 Egg Yolks
- 100g Caster Sugar
- 6 tablespoons Caster Sugar
- 45g Corn Flour

## Instructions

1. step 1.
2. Put the milk, cream, cinnamon stick and all the citrus zest in a saucepan set over a medium heat.
3. Cook, stirring often, until the milk is just steaming but not boiling, about 3-5 mins.
4. Remove from the heat, cover with a plate and leave to infuse for at least 30 mins.
5. step 2.
6. When the cream mixture has infused, whisk the egg yolks, sugar and cornflour together in a separate bowl for 3-5 mins, or until light and pale in colour.
7. Pour the infused milk through a sieve into the egg mixture, whisking continuously.
8. Return the mixture to the saucepan.
9. step 3.
10. Put the saucepan over a medium-high heat and whisk for around 10-12 mins.
11. The mixture should start thickening to a custard-like consistency – you can tell it’s ready by dipping a wooden spoon in the mixture, then running a finger through the mixture on the back of the spoon.
12. If the line holds, it's ready.
13. Sieve the mixture into a jug to remove any froth.
14. step 4.
15. Divide the custard between six 150ml ramekins or small terracotta dishes, then leave to cool for 1 hr at room temperature until set with a slight wobble.
16. Chill overnight.
17. step 5.
18. Just before serving, sprinkle 1 tbsp caster sugar over the top of each ramekin and caramelise using a kitchen blowtorch.
19. Alternatively, slide the ramekins under a hot grill until the sugar turns golden and starts to bubble.
20. Serve straightaway.
